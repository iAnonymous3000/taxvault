Before You Start

Estimate only, for a limited set of return types

This is not a filing product. Do not use these numbers to file a tax return.

Good fit for this estimate

  • Single, Married Filing Jointly, or Head of Household returns.
  • W-2 wages, SSA-1099 benefits, 1099-INT interest, and 1099-DIV dividends.
  • Standard deduction with age 65+ or blindness adjustments.
  • Known deductible IRA, HSA, and student loan interest amounts.
  • Dependents entered for child and dependent credit estimates.

Use a fuller tax product if you have

  • EIC, itemized deductions, pensions, IRA distributions, or self-employment income.
  • Capital gains, ACA credits, or most federal schedules and forms.
  • Any situation where you need filing-ready numbers or formal review.
  • Head of Household unless you already know you qualify under IRS rules.

    Manual Checks

      W-2 Wage Statements

      Add only the forms you actually have. Filled W-2 cards need an employer name, EIN, and wages.

      Field Guide

      Match the highlighted W-2 boxes before you type.

      • Box 1 goes in Wages.
      • Box 2 goes in Federal Tax Withheld.
      • Boxes 3, 4, 5, 6, and 17 only matter if you open the additional W-2 fields.

      If your paper copy is arranged a little differently, trust the printed box number.

      1099-INT Interest Statements

      Use the field guide below to match the paper form before entering the amounts.

      Field Guide

      The interest form only needs two numbers here.

      • Box 1 goes in Taxable Interest.
      • Box 8 goes in Tax-Exempt Interest.
      • Institution name is optional if you just want the estimate.

      If you have multiple 1099-INT forms, add one card for each paper form.

      SSA-1099 Social Security Benefit Statements

      Use the guide below to find the two numbers from your SSA-1099.

      Field Guide

      This form only needs the benefits box and withholding box.

      • Box 5 goes in Total Benefits.
      • Box 6 goes in Voluntary Federal Tax Withheld.
      • TaxVault computes the taxable portion for you after you enter both numbers.

      If you and your spouse each received an SSA-1099, enter one card for each form.

      1099-DIV Dividend Statements

      Use the guide below to find the two dividend boxes on your paper form.

      Field Guide

      Start with ordinary dividends, then the qualified subset.

      • Box 1a goes in Ordinary Dividends.
      • Box 1b goes in Qualified Dividends.
      • Box 1b should never be larger than Box 1a.

      If you only see ordinary dividends, you can leave qualified dividends at zero.

      Adjustments to Income

      Enter only deduction amounts you already know you can claim. TaxVault does not verify IRA coverage rules, HSA eligibility, or annual contribution limits.

      TaxVault applies the 2025 student loan interest cap and MAGI phaseout automatically.
